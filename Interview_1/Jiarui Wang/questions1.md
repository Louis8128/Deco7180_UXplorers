## Interview Questions

### Closed-ended Questions

**Q1 — Tourist Perspective**  
If this platform were fully developed, would you actively use the "Food Safety" filter when searching for restaurants? (Yes/No)

Follow-up: Why or why not? In what specific situations would this be most important to you?

**Q2 — Weight Adjustment Feature**  
Does the concept of "finding restaurants along a planned walking route" match your actual habits when traveling? (Yes/No)

Follow-up: Why? Can you give me an example of when you would prefer this over just searching "restaurants near me"?

**Q3 — Custom Route Feature**  
Would seeing an official food safety score next to a restaurant's name actually change your mind if the restaurant already had great user reviews? (Yes/No)

Follow-up: Why? How low does the safety score have to be for you to cancel a booking?

**Q4 — Interface Logic**  
Would you trust a map platform that claims to combine official government hygiene data with regular restaurant searches? (Yes/No)

Follow-up: Why or why not? What would the platform need to show to prove the data is authentic?


### Open-ended Questions

**Q5 — Trust Factor**  
Based on the basic wireframes you just navigated, did the sequence of steps (from searching to filtering to routing) feel logical to you? Was anything missing in the flow? 


**Q6 — Feature Necessity**  
Compared to how you currently find places to eat (like using Google Maps or Yelp), what do you see as the biggest potential advantage or disadvantage of using a dedicated "safe food routing" map?


**Q7 — Route Feature Value**  
Imagine the platform shows you a restaurant right on your route. It has a 5-star taste rating from users, but only a 2-star hygiene rating from the government. How would you process this information to make your final choice?


**Q8 — Interface Clarity**  
Since this is just a basic draft, what are the top two pieces of information that MUST be included in the final, fully-developed version for you to actually book a table through it?
