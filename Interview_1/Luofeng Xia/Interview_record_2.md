# User Interview Record: Food Journey Project (Participant 03)

**Date:** April 13, 2026

---

## 1. Closed-ended Questions

**Q1 – Functional Preference** Which feature do you think would be more helpful for saving your planning time: the advanced multi-filter system or the automated DIY itinerary builder?

**Yuming Zhong:** I would definitely go with the advanced multi-filter system. When I’m looking for a place to eat, I usually have a specific vibe or rating in mind, and being able to quickly narrow down the results based on popularity and high ratings is a huge time-saver.

**Follow-up:** Why do you think that specific feature is more valuable to you? In what situation would you use it the most?

**Yuming Zhong:** The filter system just feels a lot more intuitive and efficient to me. I’d mostly use it when I’m on a tight schedule—like between classes or during a busy weekend—and I just want to find a solid spot without having to sift through a bunch of irrelevant options that don't match my preferences.

⸻

**Q2 – Usage Pattern** During a short trip (e.g., 3 days), are you more likely to follow the recommended route strictly, or do you prefer to frequently adjust the plan while you are on the go?

**Yuming Zhong:** I’m the type of person who prefers to frequently adjust my plan while I’m on the go. I really don't like being stuck to a rigid, fixed schedule, especially on short trips where being flexible is half the fun.

**Follow-up:** What kind of unexpected situation (e.g., weather, fatigue, or finding a new place) usually makes you change your original plans?

**Yuming Zhong:** It’s usually things like sudden weather changes or just how much energy I have left in the tank. Also, I love the spontaneity of discovering a cool new place by accident; if I see something interesting on the street, I want to be able to shift my itinerary right then and there to check it out.

⸻

**Q3 – Workflow Preference** When using our platform, would you prefer to build a food itinerary from scratch using the DIY tool, or would you rather pick a shared route from another user and then modify it to fit your needs?

**Yuming Zhong:** I’d prefer picking a shared route and then tweaking it to fit my specific needs. It’s a great way to save time on the basic logistics while still keeping that sense of a personalized itinerary.

**Follow-up:** What would make you trust a shared route enough to use it as your starting template?

**Yuming Zhong:** I’d trust it if it has a lot of "likes" and the content is detailed. I’m always looking to see if the route feels authentic—if it looks too polished or like it was sponsored by an ad, I’ll probably keep looking for something more genuine.

⸻

**Q4 – Value Validation** Does the combination of DIY route building and referencing shared itineraries make the planning process easier and more useful for users compared to traditional methods?

**Yuming Zhong:** Definitely. Combining DIY building with shared routes makes the whole planning phase way easier. It gives you both the inspiration you need and the flexibility you want, which is something a lot of traditional methods seem to miss.

**Follow-up:** What is the biggest thing traditional methods (like Google Maps or blogs) are missing that this platform successfully provides?

**Yuming Zhong:** Traditional methods usually feel really fragmented; you’re constantly switching between different apps or blogs to get the full picture. This platform integrates everything into one spot, which makes the whole process more efficient and much easier to visualize.

⸻

## 2. Open-ended Questions

**Q5 – Trust & Social Factor** What would motivate you to share your own DIY route with other users, and what kind of details in a shared itinerary would make you trust another user’s recommendation?

**Yuming Zhong:** Some kind of incentive would be a great motivator—maybe platform recognition or even small cash rewards. For me to trust someone else's route, I look for users who show consistent activity or have some kind of verified status on the app.

**Follow-up:** If a stranger shared a route, what specific "proof" (like photos or detailed descriptions) would make it feel more authentic to you?

**Yuming Zhong:** Real photos are absolutely essential for building trust. Seeing actual, unedited images of the food and the location makes the whole itinerary feel reliable and helps me judge if the recommendations are actually worth my time.

⸻

**Q6 – Interaction Logic** If you are not satisfied with a specific restaurant in the generated route, how would you prefer to modify it (e.g., manually dragging and dropping another spot on the map, or having the system re-suggest a different one)?

**Yuming Zhong:** I’d actually like to have both options available—manual changes and system suggestions. Having that combination lets me stay flexible while still being able to rely on a quick automated recommendation when I’m feeling a bit lazy.

**Follow-up:** Why do you prefer that specific way of interacting with the map over the other?

**Yuming Zhong:** Manual editing gives me that sense of total control over my day, but system suggestions are a huge help when I just want to save effort. Having both makes the interaction feel way more adaptable to whatever situation I’m in.

⸻

**Q7 – Decision Support** When looking at the "Route 1, 2, 3" options, what key information (e.g., total walking distance, estimated waiting time at restaurants, or total cost) do you need most to help you quickly choose the best route?

**Yuming Zhong:** Total distance and the overall cost are the big ones for me. When you’re on a short trip, those two factors directly impact how convenient the trip is and whether it fits into my budget.

**Follow-up:** If two routes were almost identical, which one of those factors would be the "dealbreaker" for you?

**Yuming Zhong:** The total distance would be the absolute dealbreaker. I’m always going to go for the more efficient route that minimizes my travel time and saves me the physical effort of walking too far between spots.

⸻

**Q8 – Requirement Gathering** Besides the current options for location, safety index and route selection, what other specific factors (such as budget, group size, or dietary restrictions) would you expect the system to consider when generating your "Food Journey"?

**Yuming Zhong:** Factors like budget and group size are super important. Those two things basically dictate where you can and can't go, so they should definitely be a core part of the planning experience.

**Follow-up:** How would your planning needs change if you were traveling solo versus traveling with a group of friends?

**Yuming Zhong:** When I’m traveling solo, I’m looking for recommendations that are specifically geared toward individual dining. It’d be awesome to have a "one-click" mode that automatically adjusts the whole itinerary to be solo-travel friendly so I don't have to worry about finding places that take single diners.
