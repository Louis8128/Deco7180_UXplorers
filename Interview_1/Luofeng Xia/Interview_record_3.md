# User Interview Record 03
 
**Date:** April 13, 2026

## 1. Closed-ended Questions

**Q1 – Functional Preference** Which feature do you think would be more helpful for saving your planning time: the advanced multi-filter system or the interactive DIY itinerary builder?

**Brian Guo:** I’d definitely go with the **interactive DIY itinerary builder**. Honestly, when I’m starting to plan a trip, I usually have a rough idea of where I want to go but no clue how to connect the dots. I don't want to waste my whole afternoon manually checking distances between ten different tabs. A tool that lets me just throw my picks onto a map and organize them into a clean route is exactly what’s missing.

**Follow-up:** Why do you think that specific feature is more valuable to you? In what situation would you use it the most?

**Brian Guo:** It’s all about speed. If I’m in a new city, I want to be able to drag and drop a few spots I found into a path based on my own budget and vibe. It’s way better than building everything from zero in a notes app. I’d use this most when I’ve found a bunch of cool spots on social media and need to quickly see if they actually make sense as a single "Food Journey."

⸻

**Q2 – Usage Pattern** During a short trip (e.g., 3 days), are you more likely to follow the recommended route strictly, or do you prefer to frequently adjust the plan while you are on the go?

**Brian Guo:** I’m definitely a "frequently adjust on the go" kind of person. In my experience, things almost never go exactly how you planned them on paper anyway.

**Follow-up:** What kind of unexpected situation (e.g., weather, fatigue, or finding a new place) usually makes you change your original plans?

**Brian Guo:** It’s usually the random stuff—like if it starts pouring rain, I’m obviously going to look for places that are indoors or closer together. Or, if I’m just feeling super tired, I might ditch a far-away spot. Also, if I walk past a place that has a massive line and looks amazing, I might try to squeeze it in even if it wasn't in my original DIY plan. I need a system that makes it easy for me to pivot.

⸻

**Q3 – Workflow Preference** When using our platform, would you prefer to build a food itinerary from scratch using the DIY tool, or would you rather pick a shared route from another user and then modify it to fit your needs?

**Brian Guo:** I’d rather start with a shared route from someone else and then modify it. It feels more "pre-validated" that way. Someone else has already done the legwork of finding the good spots, which saves me a ton of time.

**Follow-up:** What would make you trust a shared route enough to use it as your starting template?

**Brian Guo:** It all comes down to credibility. If a route is just a list of names, I probably won't care. But if I see that a user has actually been there, included real photos, and left detailed notes about the experience, then I’m all in. If it looks like a real person's half-day food crawl, I’ll use that as my base and just swap out the stops I’m not into for my own choices.

⸻

**Q4 – Value Validation** Does the combination of DIY route building and referencing shared itineraries make the planning process easier and more useful for users compared to traditional methods?

**Brian Guo:** Yeah, for sure. I think the big win here is how it integrates everything. Traditional methods like Google Maps or travel blogs are so fragmented—you’re constantly jumping between tabs and trying to organize it all yourself.

**Follow-up:** What is the biggest thing traditional methods (like Google Maps or blogs) are missing that this platform successfully provides?

**Brian Guo:** It’s that "all-in-one" toolkit. Instead of me having to manually calculate distances or compare ten different blogs, this platform gives me the tools to build the plan myself in minutes. It’s focused on solving the actual problem, which is the massive time it takes to organize a route. Traditional tools give you the data, but your platform actually gives you the *workspace* to make a plan.

## 2. Open-ended Questions

**Q5 – Trust & Social Factor** What would motivate you to share your own DIY route with other users, and what kind of details in a shared itinerary would make you trust another user’s recommendation?

**Brian Guo:** I’m more likely to trust stuff that feels "real" and lifestyle-oriented rather than a generic tourist guide. I’d share my own if I thought it would actually help someone find a cool spot they’d otherwise miss.

**Follow-up:** If a stranger shared a route, what specific "proof" (like photos or detailed descriptions) would make it feel more authentic to you?

**Brian Guo:** Real food photos are a must—no studio shots. I also look for specific details like "the wait time here is actually way longer than it says online" or "this place is perfect for a group of friends." Those little personal nuggets make the route feel authentic. If a stranger includes timestamps or honest comments, I’m way more confident in their recommendations.

⸻

**Q6 – Interaction Logic** If you are not satisfied with a specific restaurant in the route you are building, how would you prefer to modify it?

**Brian Guo:** I’d definitely prefer to manually replace it using **drag-and-drop**. It just feels more intuitive to me to have that level of control.

**Follow-up:** Why do you prefer that specific way of interacting with the map over others?

**Brian Guo:** I like having a sense of control. If I can see the whole route on the map and just drag a new spot in, I can instantly see how it changes the flow of my day. I don't want a system to just guess for me; I want to be the one to fix it so it fits my exact "vibe" or the distance I'm willing to walk.

⸻

**Q7 – Decision Support** When choosing between “Route 1, 2, and 3,” what key information do you need most to help you quickly choose the best route?

**Brian Guo:** Total cost and waiting time are the big two for me. Those are the things that actually affect my mood during a trip.

**Follow-up:** If two routes were almost identical, which one of those factors would be the "dealbreaker" for you?

**Brian Guo:** Waiting time, 100%. As a student, my budget matters, but my time on a trip is even more valuable. If one route is a bit more expensive but saves me from standing in line for hours, I’m taking that one. I hate wasting half my day just waiting for a table.

⸻

**Q8 – Requirement Gathering** Besides the current options, what other specific factors would you expect the system to consider when you are generating your "Food Journey"?

**Brian Guo:** I think a **"wishlist"** feature would be huge. If I’ve already saved a bunch of places I’m interested in, the DIY builder should let me pull from that list first. It makes the whole thing feel much more personalized.

**Follow-up:** How would your planning needs change if you were traveling solo versus traveling with a group of friends?

**Brian Guo:** It’s a totally different ballgame. If I’m solo, I’m all about efficiency and being fast. But with friends, it’s more about the experience and the atmosphere. We need places that can actually fit a group and don't mind us hanging out for a while. If the DIY tool had a toggle to help me find "Group Friendly" spots while I'm building the path, that would be amazing.
