# User Interview Record 01
 
**Date:** April 13, 2026  


---

**Q1 – Functional Preference** Which feature do you think would be more helpful for saving your planning time: the advanced multi-filter system or the interactive DIY itinerary builder?

**Oliver:** I’d definitely go with the **advanced multi-filter system**. 

**Follow-up:** Why do you think that specific feature is more valuable to you? In what situation would you use it the most?

**Oliver:** Since I’m studying IT, I really appreciate when apps actually use open datasets effectively. Being able to sort by that food safety index is a huge value-add. I’d probably use it the most the second I land in a new city; I want to filter for the high-rated, safe spots first so I have a reliable "pool" of restaurants to choose from before I start DIY-ing my route.

⸻

**Q2 – Usage Pattern** During a short trip (e.g., 3 days), are you more likely to follow the recommended route strictly, or do you prefer to frequently adjust the plan while you are on the go?

**Oliver:** I am definitely the type to **frequently adjust the plan while I’m on the go**.

**Follow-up:** What kind of unexpected situation (e.g., weather, fatigue, or finding a new place) usually makes you change your original plans?

**Oliver:** It’s usually a mix of weather and just plain old fatigue. If I’ve been walking around art galleries or museums all day, my feet are going to be killing me. I’ll probably ditch a "perfect" restaurant I put in my DIY route for something decent that’s right around the corner from my hotel.

⸻

**Q3 – Workflow Preference** When using our platform, would you prefer to build a food itinerary from scratch using the DIY tool, or would you rather pick a shared route from another user and then modify it to fit your needs?

**Oliver:** I’d much rather **pick a shared route from another user and then modify it**.

**Follow-up:** What would make you trust a shared route enough to use it as your starting template?

**Oliver:** I’d trust a route if the person who shared it seems like a local or if they’ve left really specific notes—like "don't bother with the dessert here, go to the shop next door instead." I like having a "base" route to play with so I don't have to start from a totally blank map.

⸻

**Q4 – Value Validation** Does the combination of DIY route building and referencing shared itineraries make the planning process easier and more useful for users compared to traditional methods?

**Oliver:** Yeah, I think it really does. 

**Follow-up:** What is the biggest thing traditional methods (like Google Maps or blogs) are missing that this platform successfully provides?

**Oliver:** The biggest thing Google Maps misses is the "flow" of the day. It gives you a bunch of pins on a map, but it doesn't help you visualize how those places actually fit together into a logical path. Your platform turns a list of places into a DIY workspace where I can see the actual connection between spots.

⸻

**Q5 – Trust & Social Factor** What would motivate you to share your own DIY route with other users, and what kind of details in a shared itinerary would make you trust another user’s recommendation?

**Oliver:** I’d share mine if I felt like I found some "hidden gems" that other people were missing out on. To trust someone else's route, I need to see specific "pro-tips"—like which dish is the absolute must-try or what time the line starts getting crazy.

**Follow-up:** If a stranger shared a route, what specific "proof" (like photos or detailed descriptions) would make it feel more authentic to you?

**Oliver:** Authenticity is everything. I need to see raw, unfiltered photos of the food—not the professional ones from the restaurant’s own website. If the photos look like they were taken by a real person on their phone, I’m way more likely to believe the review is genuine.

⸻

**Q6 – Interaction Logic** If you are not satisfied with a specific restaurant in the route you are building, how would you prefer to modify it?

**Oliver:** I’d definitely prefer **manually dragging and dropping** another spot.

**Follow-up:** Why do you prefer that specific way of interacting with the map over others?

**Oliver:** I like having that direct control. Dragging and dropping feels much more intuitive because I can see exactly how the new spot changes the walking distance or the overall flow of my afternoon without having to wait for the system to guess what I want.

⸻

**Q7 – Decision Support** When looking at the "Route 1, 2, 3" options, what key information (e.g., total walking distance, estimated waiting time at restaurants, or total cost) do you need most to help you quickly choose the best route?

**Oliver:** I need to see the **estimated waiting time** and the **total walking distance** right at the top of the route card.

**Follow-up:** If two routes were almost identical, which one of those factors would be the "dealbreaker" for you?

**Oliver:** Total wait time is the absolute dealbreaker for me. If I’m on a short holiday, I don't want to spend three hours of my day standing on a sidewalk. If one route saves me an hour of waiting, I’m taking that one every single time.

⸻

**Q8 – Requirement Gathering** Besides the current options for location, safety index and route selection, what other specific factors (such as budget, group size, or dietary restrictions) would you expect the system to consider when you are building your "Food Journey"?

**Oliver:** I’d expect the DIY tool to consider **budget** and **group size**.

**Follow-up:** How would your planning needs change if you were traveling solo versus traveling with a group of friends?

**Oliver:** If I’m solo, I’m looking for quick counter seats or "solo-friendly" spots. But if I’m out with a group of friends from uni, the vibe is totally different—we need a place with big tables where we can actually sit for a while and talk without being rushed out by the staff.
